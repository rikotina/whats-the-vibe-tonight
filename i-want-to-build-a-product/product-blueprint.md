# Product Blueprint

## MVP promise

Tell the app your mood, outfit, budget, neighborhood, and social energy. It returns three Tokyo options:

- Best match
- Slightly bolder
- Easy fallback

## User flow

1. Open the app.
2. Pick a mood.
3. Add optional practical filters.
4. Review three recommendations.
5. Open details, save, or share the URL.
6. Tune the night with quick actions like quieter, bolder, or lower budget.

## Design direction

The interface should feel editorial, nocturnal, calm, and practical. It should avoid travel-guide clutter and nightlife-bro energy. The copy should make taste explicit: why this place fits this exact night, when to avoid it, and what the next move could be.

## Data launch checklist

- Replace demo entries with 100 verified Tokyo venues.
- Add real addresses, coordinates, map URLs, booking links, and photos.
- Verify opening hours and reservation friction every 30 days.
- Add at least 6 editorial fields per venue.
- Collect simple feedback: nailed it, too expensive, too loud, too far, not my vibe, closed or wrong.

## 30/60/90 launch plan

First 30 days: curate Shibuya, Ebisu, Nakameguro, Daikanyama, Ginza, and Kagurazaka. Launch a waitlist and test the quiz manually with friends in Tokyo.

First 60 days: publish the mobile web MVP with 100 verified venues, saved places, shareable result links, and analytics on which moods produce clicks.

First 90 days: add weekly editorial drops, richer itineraries, and a lightweight concierge request flow for users who want the app to plan a full night.
